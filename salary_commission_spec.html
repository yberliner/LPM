Salary Commission Calculation

LPM System — Financial Specification Document — April 2026

1. Overview

The salary report calculates staff earnings for a selected date range. It has three components:

A. Session Salary — Auditor pay for auditing sessions (existing)
B. CS Review Salary — Case Supervisor pay for reviews (existing)
C. Commission Earnings — Staff commissions from purchase payments (NEW)

This document details component C — how commission earnings are calculated from payments that have been confirmed as "Money in the Bank".

2. Data Source: Which Payments Count?

Only payments where:
  • IsMoneyInBank = 1 (admin has confirmed the money arrived)
  • MoneyInBankDate falls within the salary report's From – To date range

Source table: fin_payment_methods, linked to fin_purchases → fin_purchase_items

3. The Deduction Chain

Every qualifying payment goes through a deduction chain before commissions are calculated:

1 Start with Gross Amount — fin_payment_methods.Amount (VAT-inclusive)
2 Subtract VAT — Price includes VAT, so we extract it:
vatAmount = Gross − (Gross / (1 + VatPct/100))
3 Subtract CC Commission — Only for CreditCard payments:
ccAmount = Gross × (CcCommissionPct/100)     (0 if Cash/Check/etc.)
4 Net After Deductions:
netAfterDeductions = Gross − vatAmount − ccAmount
5 Subtract Reserve Deduction — Applied on the net after ALL deductions:
reserveAmount = netAfterDeductions × (ReserveDeductPct/100)
6 Final Net Base — This is the amount commissions are calculated on:
NET BASE = netAfterDeductions − reserveAmount
Example: Payment of ₪1,180 via CreditCard, VAT=18%, CC=2.5%, Reserve=10%

VAT = 1180 − (1180 / 1.18) = 1180 − 1000 = ₪180
CC = 1180 × 2.5% = ₪29.50
Net after deductions = 1180 − 180 − 29.50 = ₪970.50
Reserve = 970.50 × 10% = ₪97.05
Net Base = ₪873.45

4. Commission Categories

Purchase items are classified into three categories, each with different commission rules:

Category 1: Auditing & Books

fin_purchase_items.ItemType = 'Auditing' or 'Book'

For each "in the bank" payment within the date range, calculate net base, then:
Role Source Commission
REGISTRAR fin_purchases.RegistrarId AuditRegistrarPct % of Net Base
REFERRAL fin_purchases.ReferralId AuditReferralPct % of Net Base

Category 2: PC Courses

ItemType = 'Course' where lkp_courses.CourseType = 'PC'

Registrar & Referral — same as above but with course percentages:
Role Source Commission
REGISTRAR fin_purchases.RegistrarId CourseRegistrarPct % of Net Base
REFERRAL fin_purchases.ReferralId CourseReferralPct % of Net Base

Academy Instructor Commissions (from config — up to 4 instructors):
Type When Payments Counted Commission
Start Commission On every "in the bank" payment within the date range Only payments with MoneyInBankDate in range Per-instructor StartPct % of Net Base
Finish Commission Only if acad_student_courses.DateFinished is within the date range ALL past "in the bank" payments for this purchase (regardless of date) Per-instructor FinishPct % of Net Base

Category 3: OT Courses (One-on-One Training)

ItemType = 'Course' where lkp_courses.CourseType = 'OT'

Registrar & Referral — same as PC courses:
Role Source Commission
REGISTRAR fin_purchases.RegistrarId CourseRegistrarPct % of Net Base
REFERRAL fin_purchases.ReferralId CourseReferralPct % of Net Base

OT-specific commissions — ONLY when course is finished within date range:
Role Source Commission
INSTRUCTOR acad_student_courses.InstructorId InstructorOtPct % of Net Base on ALL past payments
CS acad_student_courses.CsId CsOtPct % of Net Base on ALL past payments

Academy Instructors (the 4 config slots) receive NO commission on OT courses.

6. Special Cases

Referral = "Other" (ReferralId = -1)

When a purchase has ReferralId = -1, the referral commission is calculated but not assigned to any staff member. Instead, it is displayed at the bottom of the salary report as an unassigned referral amount, together with the fin_purchases.Notes field, so the admin knows who to pay manually.

Payment Allocation Across Items

A single payment method amount covers the entire purchase, which may contain multiple items (e.g., Auditing hours + a Course + a Book). The payment amount is distributed proportionally across items based on their AmountPaid relative to the purchase total:

itemShare = paymentAmount × (item.AmountPaid / totalPurchaseAmount)

Each item's share then goes through the deduction chain independently, and the appropriate commission percentages are applied based on the item's category.
